Create a school garden

  • Students can participate in the creation of the garden. To direct them correctly, there are some aspects to consider, such as:
  • Tools needed to work the land: pick, rake, shovels, watering can, hose and gardening gloves.
  • Choose a piece of land that is ventilated and with enough light.
  • Prepare the soil, eliminating weeds or stones

Why?

A school garden is a plot of land within the school, where students plant, grow and harvest vegetables and greens. It is about having a space in the school so that students can experience and know the natural cycle of the plants that provide food, promote their learning and expand their training. The students will learn with a school garden to get involved in the cultivation and for this, they will learn lessons about nature and food, to work as a team and encourage fellowship.

Importance of a school garden

The learning of the students is very enriching in all aspects, which brings some advantages. The cultivation of native products of the city or country is enhanced. Students work and play in a natural space, interacting with their immediate surroundings. Values ​​of respect, conservation and care of nature, the environment and the environment are incorporated.

Do not compost

Cooked food, coal and coke ash, meat and fish, bones, cat litter, dog poo, disposable nappies or human poo, glossy paper, weeds, woody stems, or diseased plants.
